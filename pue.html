Building awareness with Police Unions Exposed
Creating a responsive website to educate on social injustices within police unions

UX Research, Information Architecture, UI Design

Background

Did you know that since 2006, 25% of American officers fired by police chiefs for misconduct were eventually reinstated after police unions appeals? Neither did I.
Police Unions Exposed is a non-profit organization that highlights and fights for social justice and accountability in police unions. They wanted to build a responsive website around their mission to educate their readers and incite reform through petitions.

Business Goals:
  1. Educate readers about police unions and their impacts on policy, regulations, and people’s lives
  2. Increase petition signatures for their core campaigns
  3. Promote the joining of their newsletter and social media
Approach

Role: UX designer

Timeline: Oct. 2020 - Dec. 2020

Tools: Figma, Google surveys


Design Solution

User Research

Discovering engagement in an agile framework

We were pressed for time to push out a design for the dev team. Using an agile framework, I suggested we send out a survey to see how people stay engaged when reading information-dense websites. We also collected people's thoughts on an initial homepage mockup provided to us by our stakeholders.

What keeps people engaged while reading?

Why do people enjoy their method of following the news?

  1. Convenience
    2. People enjoyed not having to deliberately seek for the news. With notifications or informative headlines, they could quickly scan for important information.
    How might we make getting information easier?
  2. Unbiased
    3. People looked up various sources to fact check their information and view opposing perspectives. They felt that with all sides of the story, they could form their own opinion.
    How might we promote the reliability and trust of the website?
