01. Overview

Background

Northville Rehabilitation Centre is a multidisciplinary clinic located in Markham, ON. This is the second location that the owners have opened, with the first location having been in operation for over 30 years. Though word of mouth is currently their primary source of referrals, Northville would like to expand their marketing efforts and audience demographic.

Details

Role: UX/UI Designer
Timeline: 4 weeks
Tools Used: Figma, OptimalSort, wix.com
Skills: UX Research, Content Copywriting, Responsive design

Design Opportunity

General research on the health behaviours of "millenials" (those born between 1986 to 1996) showed that over 55% research for their healthcare information online and trust it just as much as their doctor. They are also twice as likely to have a more holistic approach to health, with 71% citing health and wellness to include more than just "not feeling sick". This opened up the opportunity to educate the young demographic on the benefits of rehabiliative therapy.

Design Solution

In keeping with the habits of the young millenial demographic, I redesigned Northville's website on their current platform, wix.com. However, I recognized that a pretty website would do little to make them unique from the dozens of other local rehabiliation clinics. Thus, I also decided to revamp their content strategy based on the research conducted. By making the content educational and engaging, I hoped to reduce the communication barrier between the general public and rehabiliation industry.

Assessing the impact of different layouts on user behaviours

Users were given tasks to complete on three existing clinic websites. I wanted to see how much of an impact different website layouts would have on a user’s behaviour and thought processes when completing the tasks. I also hoped to solidify any patterns that might’ve emerged, and give the user a chance to figure out what factors they prioritized in their decision when choosing a clinic.
To test my assumptions, I created a set of tasks based on the three most frequently asked questions I encountered during my working clinic experience.

  4. Users expect clear guidance on how to take action
  5. Practitioner biographies do not hold as high of a priority as services information
  6. Information must be easy to find and match people’s mental models

04. Design

Content Copywriting

From the user research, it was clear that a copywriting revamp was needed on the website.

Before doing any writing, I first established the user’s goals and motivations for wanting to read any clinic service information.

Goal: Users wanted to choose the appropriate type of therapy to book with.
Need: In order to accomplish this, they had to read and learn about the offered services.

I referenced Mailchimp’s fantastic style guide and wrote out four questions to focus on:

  1. What is the service
  2. How do they carry out their treatment
  3. What do they treat
  4. What benefits does their treatment provide?

By focusing on answering these four questions, I hoped to provide clarity to prospective users in a concise and digestible format. To match Northville’s brand attributes, the content was written in a professional and plainspoken tone.

06. Improvements

Task #1: Find the common conditions treated by chiropractic

In the beginning, it was hard for users to keep their eye engaged on the discipline details page because of the amount of whitespace around the elements. In the iterations, I was mindful about this and made sure to better use the page's real-estate. I made layout changes and utlized the concept of the user's eye travelling in a introduced a second column for the "common conditions". This took advantage of the user's natural eye movement in the "F" pattern and kept them engaged while reading.

Task #2: Find out if they accept private insurance

Though all the users ended up completing the task, there were several barriers along the way. They disliked scrolling through information that was irrelevant to their task. They also expressed being visually overwhelmed by a section full of insurance logos. "I'd like the option to ctrl+f my insurance company to find out if the clinic works with them" - user comment.
I took this feedback and decided to create a separate page with a list of all the insurance providers that the clinic work with.

Task #3: Try to book an appointment

This task proved to be the trickiest out of all three. All three participants had different ideas of how to complete the task, and each encountered different problems. The footer CTA to book an appointment was not noticed, and the wording caused users to expect an online booking feature. If they didn't see the footer, users went to the services page thinking they'd be able to book an appointment after choosing a service or discipline. The copywriting on the contact page felt redundant to users, and provided no actual guidance on how to book an appointment. As a result, I was compelled to re-examine the user flows I had initially created based on the research. It was here where I noted all the different points at which a user would consider booking an appointment. Coupled with observing how people approached the task during the usability testing, it became obvious that I needed to incorporate more opportunities for people to book an appointment. The second round of usability testing was a huge win, because all users completed the task with no issues (yay!).

Layout

While the first round of testing was mainly focused on task flows, the second round showed that improvements needed to be made to the UI. The colour palette I used on the site was originally only Northville’s signature brand colour of royal blue. Though it really stayed showed a professional brand attribute, users felt offput and couldn't connect with the clinic.
In the iterated version (right), I used warmer tones that would make the users feel more comfortable while navigating an information-dense site. I also hoped that the warmer yellows and blues would help further instill brand trust within the clinic.

07. Learnings

Redesigning the website for my old clinic brought me back all kinds of nostalgia from my early career. I loved being able to accomplish one of my dreams of returning to the healthcare industry through a design lens to improve a patients' user experience. I hope that the redesign helps educate the younger demographic on the benefits of rehabiliative therapy and maintenance care to keep your physical body in tip top shape!

1. Function over form
I learned that you can have valuable information on a website, but if it's not designed or organized in an engaging format, users aren't going to notice it. With Mailchimp's help, I had a crash course on how to create concise content that educates the user without overwhelming them. I was given the opportunity to observe how users navigate (or don't navigate) information-dense sites, and how bullet points, headers, and use of whitespace can keep a user engaged.

2. Multiple Flows
We learn that people have different mental models when approaching a task. However, seeing it in practice with my design helped me understand that there doesn't have to be one flow us designers create to accomplish a task. The essence of UX is to create an experience that is frictionless to those who use the product. I was stuck in the mindset that I had to create one optimal flow to complete a task, but this showed me that I can provide multiple opportunities throughout the website to cater to different ways of thinking.

3. Power of colour theory
It was great to incorporate Northville's brand colour throughout the site, but that stark colour contrast is what most likely limited the site in its branding. The clinic looked one dimensional in only giving the vibe of professionalism, and it didn't connect with the users. By incorporating the warmer tones, users felt more comfortable while navigating and would be more inclined to trust this clinic with their healthcare.
