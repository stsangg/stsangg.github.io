Background

Northville Rehabilitation Centre is a newly opened rehabiliation clinic located in Markham, ON. They would like to expand their audience demographic, but find it difficult through their current source of referrals: word of mouth. The redesign of their website will increase their online presence to market themselves within the local community.

Details

Role: UX/UI Designer
Timeline: 4 weeks
Tools Used: Figma, OptimalSort, wix.com

Approach
Design Opportunity

As a new and local rehabilitation clinic, Northville’s goal is to increase their number of walk-ins and referrals. With over 55% of millenials researching their healthcare online (and finding it just as reliable as a doctor), Northville hopes to establish its online presence and align its values with this younger demographic and the community.

Design Solution

By creating an educational and responsive website, Northville hopes to capture the younger millenial demographic. Revamped copywriting will help succintly inform website visitors on offered clinic services and their benefits. Coupled with an intuitive informational architecture, website visitors can easily navigate around and make an informed decision about their health.

Research

Identifying common thought and behaviour patterns

Participants navigated three different clinic websites to identify common thought and behaviour patterns for navigation. This also helped show what factors people considered important when choosing a clinic.

Method: remote task-based contextual inquiry


Tasks were based on my assumptions of a patient's most frequently asked questions:

  • What is the difference between physiotherapy and chiropractic treatment?
  • Do you accept private insurance?
  • How do I book an appointment?

I created a word map to identify the common thought and behvaiour patterns. The larger the word, the more prevalent the pattern amongst users.

From here, I knew I needed to focus on three key things:

  1. Keywords - used to navigate text-heavy pages
  2. Copywriting - needs to be simple and understandable
  3. Guidance - instruct on how to take action (ie. booking an appointment)

From the collective participant data, I created an empathy map to remind me of the expectations of the types of user I was designing for.

Competitor task flow comparison

What’re the best practices in existing sites?

I evaluated four random clinic websites to discover their organization tactics and best practices. I used the top three factors people considered when choosing a clinic:

  1. Learning about the offered services
  2. Whether they accepted their insurance company
  3. Ease of booking an appointment


Competitor takeaways

  1. All the sites had their offered services under “services” in the global navigation
  2. There is no consistent placement for insurance information
  3. If clinics didn't offer online booking, the booking information was under a section related to "contact"
Detailed Research Analysis

Content Copywriting

Going back to a user's purpose

Taking a content-first approach, I went back to the purpose of why people would read the information.


People want to choose the appropriate type of therapy to book with. In order to accomplish this, they have to understand the offered services.



I referenced Mailchimp’s fantastic style guide and focused on answering these questions for a concise and easily digestible copywriting.

  1. What is the service?
  2. How do they carry out their treatment?
  3. What do they treat?
  4. What benefits does their treatment provide?

To match Northville’s brand attributes, I wrote in a professional and plainspoken tone.

Remote card sorting analysis

Addressing information architecture assumptions

In keeping with the business' goals, it was important to create an intuitive architecture. Unfortunately, the competitor analysis didn't show consistent patterns, so I asked the users directly through a cardsorting activity. I wanted to find out where people would look for:

  1. Clinic details
  2. Insurance information

Takeaways:

  1. “Contact” would be the most likely place people would look to find clinic details
  2. “Private insurance” or "Extended Health Coverage" are interchangeable terms, and “services” is the most likely place people would look.

Usability testing - round 1

Testing the flow

For the first round of testing, I created a prototype from my mid-fidelity wireframes to make sure that the task flows were intuitive.

My goals were to:

  • Assess the mid-fidelity layout
  • Evaluate the effectiveness and/or potential barriers to proposed task flows

Measuring success:

  1. > 70% of users complete the task
  2. < 70% of users are confused

Visualizing improvements

For each round of testing, I created affinity map and organized the user's data based on tasks. This helped me to visually identify the most common, and therefore highest priority, problems. The black boxes around each card represent small wins. The second round of testing showed a 72% increase in black boxes, meaning more wins! :)

Design iterations - round 2

To improve the booking flow, I noted the different points at which a user would consider booking and observed the following steps they took to complete the task. As this was important to conversion metrics and business goals, I incorporated more opportunities throughout for people to book an appointment. The second round of testing was a huge win, because 100% users completed the task with no issues!




Assessing the UI

This round showed that improvements needed to be made to the UI. People were offput from seeing Northville's royal blue brand color so often throughout the site, and they felt they couldn’t connect with the clinic. In the iterated version (right), I used warmer tones that would complement the brand colour and make the people feel more comfortable while navigating an information-dense site. The warmer yellows and blues would also help instill brand trust for the clinic.

Learnings

Final thoughts

I loved being able to take a step towards my goal of returning to the healthcare industry through a design focus to improve the patient experience. I hope that the redesign helps educate my generation on the benefits of rehabiliative and maintenace therapy to keep the physical body in tip top shape!

1. Function over form
Valuable information can live on a website, but it doesn't necessarily mean users are reading it. With Mailchimp's help, I learned how to create succinct content that can educate without overwhelming. Through observation, I saw how design practices like bullet points, whitespace, and bolded words make an impact on user behaviour and navigation.

2. Power of colour theory
Though beautiful, Northville's initial colour palette had stark contrast and made the clinic appear one dimensional to incoming users. It only gave the vibe of professionalism, and failed to connect with the users. It was really interesting to see how warmer tones really made an impact for users to feel more comfortable and trustworthy towards this clinic.
