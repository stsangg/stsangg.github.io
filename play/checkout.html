Ready to checkout?

Rather than recreating the typical checkout page, I wanted to have a little more fun with the design. I was envisioning a hypebeast-esque, cool company. To reflect this, I opted for a simple black and white color scheme and used a bright yellow as the highlighting color to draw attention. A big bold font with untraditional header placements fit in perfectly with the brand, and emphasized the ~cool and different~ factor that so many hypebeast brands convey.
Despite all of this, I couldn't forget the basics. See my thought process below.

When are people using checkout screens?

To purchase an item. Checkout screens are an area for people to put in the following information:

  • payment
  • billing
  • shipping
What do people need to see?
  • summary of the items they're buying
  • account information to trace the purchase
  • cost breakdown
