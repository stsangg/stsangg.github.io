Ready to checkout?

Rather than recreating the typical checkout page, I wanted to have a little more fun with the design. I started off with the basics of what was needed to make a successful checkout screen, and then later incorproated the branding.

Thought Process


When are people using checkout screens?

To purchase an item. Checkout screens are an area for people to put in the following information:

  • payment
  • billing
  • shipping

What do people need to see?

  • summary of the items they're buying
  • account information to trace the purchase
  • cost breakdown

Ideating

I tinkered around with a few layouts before landing on the idea of an untraditional header placement. A sticky summary section on the right would've been great for users to see the items and cost breakdown as they enter their information.

Branding

At this point, I was envisioning a hypebeast-esque, cool company. I opted for a simple black and white color scheme and used a bright yellow as the highlighting color to draw attention. Pairing the vertical headers with a big bold font really emphasized the ~cool and different~ factor that so many of these brands convey.
